Cybersecurity

Redact Labs' cybersecurity practice delivers threat protection, endpoint security, and continuous monitoring to Canadian businesses, aligned to NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0 and CIS Controls v8.1. Every programme is scoped by a senior engineer and built to match the client's real risk — not a vendor checklist.

Cybersecurity at Redact Labs means a structured, standards-aligned programme covering five areas: identity and access, endpoint protection, email and data security, monitoring and detection, and incident preparedness. We don't sell tools; we build the programme around the tools that fit your environment.

What a Redact Labs cybersecurity engagement includes

Typical scope

  • Multi-factor authentication rolled out across email, banking, critical business systems, and file storage
  • Endpoint protection (Microsoft Defender for Business or Bitdefender GravityZone) deployed and centrally managed
  • Email security hardening — SPF, DKIM, DMARC configured and monitored; advanced threat protection where licensing permits
  • Backup & recovery — independent, tested backups of Microsoft 365 / Google Workspace data
  • DNS filtering to block malware and phishing domains at the network layer
  • Security awareness training (KnowBe4 or equivalent) — monthly micro-training and quarterly phishing simulations
  • Continuous monitoring via Managed Detection and Response (MDR) for mid-tier and above engagements

How we scope an engagement

Every cybersecurity programme starts with a security audit or Posture Snapshot. The audit tells us where you actually are — not where a generic maturity model assumes you are. From that baseline, we write a fixed-fee Statement of Work covering the first 30, 60, and 90 days, with a clear monthly managed-service fee thereafter.

We operate on three principles:

  1. Senior-only delivery. The engineer scoping your programme is the same engineer delivering it. No handoffs, no junior rotation.
  2. Vendor-agnostic recommendations. We'll work with the tools you have wherever sensible, and we recommend new tools only where they genuinely fit — not because we're on a partner kickback.
  3. Plain-English reporting. Every quarterly review includes a one-page executive summary your board or owner can read in 90 seconds.

Standards and frameworks we align to

  • NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0 — the six-function model (Govern, Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover)
  • CIS Critical Security Controls v8.1 — Implementation Groups 1, 2, and 3
  • CyberSecure Canada — federal certification readiness (13 baseline controls)
  • PIPEDA, Quebec Law 25, Ontario PHIPA — Canadian privacy and health-information compliance
  • ISO/IEC 27001 and SOC 2 — readiness preparation for organisations pursuing certification

Pricing

Our managed cybersecurity service starts at $750/month for small businesses (up to 15 users) and scales with size and complexity. One-time security audits are fixed-fee from $2,500. We publish indicative pricing on every audit report — no vague "contact us for a quote" emails.
