// About

Threat-aware. Operationally sharp.

Redact Labs was founded in 2025 to bridge enterprise-grade cybersecurity practices with the real operational needs of growing Canadian businesses, charities, and individuals.

Why we exist

The modern digital threat landscape has outpaced what most Canadian organisations can handle in-house. Small and mid-sized businesses, charities, and event producers all face the same attacker techniques that enterprises face — but without the enterprise security budget. At the same time, the pure-play cybersecurity firms that do exist tend to either (a) price themselves out of reach for smaller clients, or (b) deliver by junior analysts following a checklist.

Redact Labs was built to close that gap. Every engagement is led by a senior engineer. Every scope is written down before work starts. Every report is readable by a non-technical board. And every tool recommendation is made on the merits, not on partner kickbacks.

What we do well

  • Cybersecurity programmes for Canadian SMBs and charities — structured, standards-aligned, and sized to the client's real risk
  • Security audits that produce useful written reports, not shelfware
  • Event IT and AV production for concerts, tours, conferences, and venues — spun up fast, torn down clean
  • Strategic IT advisory that helps clients make significant technology decisions without vendor bias
  • Charity-sector work at every size, from a three-person grassroots charity to a mid-sized foundation

How we work

Operating principles

  • Senior-only delivery. The engineer scoping your work is the one delivering it.
  • Fixed-fee engagements. You see the scope, price, and deliverables before you sign.
  • Vendor-agnostic. We recommend what fits, not what pays us.
  • Plain-English reporting. Every quarterly review has a board-ready one-page summary.
  • Direct communication. You have your engineer's mobile number. No ticket-desk labyrinth.

Who we work with

Our work concentrates in three areas:

  1. Sports and entertainment. Major Toronto-area sports franchises, MLSE-associated venues, Live Nation touring productions, premium Canadian event venues, and international touring acts.
  2. Charities and nonprofits. A dedicated five-tier practice for Canadian registered charities — purpose-built around donor-data protection, PIPEDA compliance, and board-level reporting.
  3. Small and mid-market businesses. Growing organisations (10–200 staff) that have outgrown ad-hoc IT but can't justify a full internal cybersecurity team.

What we don't do

We believe transparency about our limits builds more trust than a marketing page claiming we do everything:

  • We don't sell software or hardware as a reseller. We'll help you procure, but we're not on the margin.
  • We don't do penetration testing in-house. We coordinate with vetted partner firms.
  • We don't provide legal or tax advice. We know good lawyers and accountants and refer generously.
  • We don't issue formal compliance certifications (ISO 27001, SOC 2). We prepare clients for them.

Compliance frameworks

Redact Labs regularly delivers work aligned to:

  • NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0
  • CIS Critical Security Controls v8.1 (IG1, IG2, IG3)
  • CyberSecure Canada (federal certification readiness)
  • PIPEDA (Canadian federal privacy)
  • Quebec Law 25 (formerly Bill 64)
  • Ontario PHIPA (personal health information)
  • ISO/IEC 27001 readiness
  • SOC 2 readiness
