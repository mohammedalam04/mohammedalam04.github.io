FAQ

Answers to the questions we receive most often, from first-time inquiries through to detailed service scoping. If your question isn't here, just email us.

What is Redact Labs?

Redact Labs is a Toronto-based cybersecurity, IT infrastructure, and audio-visual services firm founded in 2025. We deliver enterprise-grade security, managed IT, and large-scale event AV to sports and entertainment clients, charities, and small-to-medium businesses across Canada and the United States.

Where is Redact Labs located?

Redact Labs is headquartered in Toronto, Ontario, Canada. We serve clients across Canada and the United States. Our primary market is the Greater Toronto Area, but we travel for event engagements and deliver remote work for clients nationally.

What services does Redact Labs offer?

We offer eight core services: cybersecurity, security auditing, IT consultation, networking, event IT, A/V panel setup and programming, service desk support, and personal cybersecurity for individuals. See our full service list for details on each.

How much does a cybersecurity audit cost?

Redact Labs security audits are fixed-fee based on organisation size: from $1,500 for individuals, $2,500 for small organisations (up to 15 users), and $5,000 for mid-market (15–75 users). Larger organisations are quoted on request. For registered Canadian charities, our Posture Snapshot is a $497 purpose-built entry offer.

How much does managed cybersecurity cost?

Managed cybersecurity services at Redact Labs start at $750/month for small businesses (up to 15 users) and scale based on organisation size and complexity. Mid-tier (Secure) and top-tier (Guardian) managed services add MDR, vulnerability management, and fractional CISO hours, typically ranging from $2,000 to $10,000/month.

Do you work with charities and nonprofits?

Yes. Redact Labs operates a dedicated charity cybersecurity practice with five published service tiers: Posture Snapshot ($497), Tier 0 Ground Zero Stabilisation (4-week, $3,000–$15,000), Essentials, Secure, and Guardian. The practice is designed around the operational realities of Canadian registered charities, including PIPEDA, Quebec Law 25, and Ontario PHIPA compliance.

What compliance frameworks do you work with?

Redact Labs aligns engagements to NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0, CIS Critical Security Controls v8.1 (IG1, IG2, IG3), CyberSecure Canada, PIPEDA, Quebec Law 25, and Ontario PHIPA. We also prepare clients for ISO/IEC 27001 and SOC 2 readiness, though we do not issue certifications ourselves.

Do you have experience with major event productions?

Yes. Our event IT and AV practice has delivered for major Toronto sports franchises, MLSE-associated venues, Live Nation touring productions, and premium Toronto event venues. We are currently delivering multi-city AV panel programming for touring productions across Canadian and US venues.

What makes Redact Labs different from other IT firms?

Three things: (1) Senior-only delivery — the engineer who scopes your engagement is the one delivering it, with no handoffs to juniors. (2) Fixed-fee transparency — scope, price, and deliverables are written down before you sign. (3) Unusually broad domain coverage — we're one of few firms that can run a corporate cyber programme and simultaneously spin up a 500-person event's entire IT stack in 48 hours.

How quickly can you respond to an inquiry?

New inquiries receive a response within four business hours during our standard hours (Monday to Friday, 9 AM to 6 PM Eastern). Urgent security incidents from existing managed-service clients are covered by the contractual SLA — typically 4 hours to first contact, 24/7 for Secure and Guardian tier clients.

Do you resell software or hardware?

No. Redact Labs is vendor-agnostic. We recommend what fits your organisation and budget, not what pays us a partner commission. We'll happily help you procure through your preferred reseller or directly from the vendor.

Do you offer penetration testing?

Not directly. Penetration testing requires a specialised methodology and certification regime we choose not to carry in-house. Where a client needs a formal pentest, we coordinate with vetted partner firms and manage the engagement, including scoping, findings review, and remediation planning. A Redact Labs security audit is a different product — it is a diagnostic, not an exploitation test.

Can you help with cyber insurance applications?

Yes. We help clients understand what their underwriter is actually asking for, document existing controls, close identified gaps, and prepare the application responses. This is a common consulting engagement — underwriter requirements have tightened significantly in recent years, and many organisations discover gaps they didn't know they had during the application process.

What happens in a security incident?

Managed-service clients have a documented incident-response plan and a 24/7 phone line. For any suspected incident, the first call is to Redact Labs' on-call engineer. We triage, contain, and coordinate — including engaging specialist incident-response firms for confirmed breaches. Non-clients can still reach us for emergency response; we'll engage on an hourly basis and recommend appropriate next steps, including regulatory notifications where applicable.

Do you work with clients outside Canada?

Yes. We serve clients across the United States, particularly for event IT and touring production work. For managed cybersecurity services, we prefer Canadian clients due to proximity and regulatory familiarity, but we'll consider US engagements on a case-by-case basis.

How do you handle confidentiality?

Every engagement starts with a signed NDA. Client data is accessed only on a need-to-know basis by the assigned engineer. Communication is via end-to-end-encrypted channels where sensitive. We do not publicly disclose client names without explicit permission, which is why most client references on this site are sector-level rather than by name.

Who founded Redact Labs?

Redact Labs was founded in 2025 by a technical leader with over a decade of experience in enterprise cybersecurity, IT infrastructure, and large-scale event production — previously delivering work for major Toronto-area sports franchises, international touring productions, and premium Canadian venues. See the About page for more.

What does the name "Redact Labs" mean?

The name reflects the core function of security work: deciding what to expose and what to protect. The [REDACT] brand element — a word struck through — is a visual metaphor for information control, which is the foundation of both cybersecurity and privacy engineering.

Can I book a free consultation?

Yes. Initial scoping calls (30–45 minutes) are always free. We use them to understand what you need and decide together whether a Redact Labs engagement is the right fit. Reach out to book one.

Do you have references?

Yes, available on request after an initial scoping call. Most of our references are sector-based (for example, a charity client speaking to another charity) to preserve client confidentiality while giving you a useful conversation.
