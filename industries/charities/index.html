Charity Cybersecurity Practice

Redact Labs operates a dedicated five-tier cybersecurity practice for Canadian registered charities — purpose-built around donor-data protection, sector-specific compliance (PIPEDA, Quebec Law 25, Ontario PHIPA), and reporting your board can actually read.

The Redact Labs charity practice is a structured, five-tier cybersecurity service model built specifically for Canadian registered charities. Every tier is fixed-fee or fixed-scope. Every engagement is led by a senior engineer. Every report is written in plain English for a non-technical board. The practice exists because charities face the same threats as corporations — phishing, ransomware, donor-data breaches — but with a fraction of the budget and none of the internal IT capacity.

Why charities specifically?

Three reasons the charity sector needs purpose-built cybersecurity, not scaled-down enterprise services:

  • Donor data is unusually sensitive. A donor list is a target-rich database: names, giving capacity, contact details, sometimes estate-planning context. The reputational damage of a leak is direct — a 2024 Canadian sector survey found ~28% of donors say they would stop giving to a charity that suffered a breach.
  • Operating budgets don't allow for large IT contracts. Most Canadian charities operate with a small admin overhead. A $50,000 cybersecurity programme is unrealistic; a $497 entry offer with a clear path to managed service is.
  • The starting posture is usually "ground zero". Most charities have no MFA, no documented policies, donor data spread across spreadsheets and personal Gmail accounts, and no named person responsible for cyber. The work needed is structured and sequential, not complex.

What the Posture Snapshot looks like

The Posture Snapshot is the entry point. It's a fixed $497 offer that produces an 8–12 page written report your board can read in 90 seconds via the executive summary and dig into for the rest of the afternoon. It includes:

Posture Snapshot deliverables

  • 90-minute structured interview with up to three people at the charity
  • 30-point external scan of public-facing infrastructure — nothing invasive, all from the outside like an attacker would see
  • Written report: executive summary, risk heat-map (3×3 impact × likelihood), top 10 findings ranked and explained in plain English
  • NIST CSF 2.0 current-state profile across all six functions (Govern, Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover)
  • CIS IG1 gap analysis (rough percentage of the 56 IG1 safeguards currently met)
  • 30/60/90-day plan with specific first actions you can take at zero cost
  • Specific Tier 0 quote, valid 60 days
  • Plain-English glossary for any term used
  • Optional 30-minute readout meeting

The Snapshot is intentionally designed to be useful even if the charity never spends another dollar with us — the report alone gives you a written, ranked, prioritised view of your risk that you can act on independently.

What Tier 0 does in four weeks

Tier 0 is where most of the transformational work happens. At the end of four weeks, the charity has seven specific deliverables:

  1. Ground-Zero Posture Report — before-and-after NIST CSF profile
  2. Acceptable Use Policy — one-page, board-approved, signed by all staff and volunteers
  3. Master Contact List — donor information consolidated into one source of truth
  4. Access Inventory — every system, every user, dormant accounts deactivated
  5. MFA Enrolment Report — multi-factor authentication on email, banking, donor database, file storage, and payment processor
  6. Incident Phone Tree — printed, laminated, posted, with top numbers tested
  7. 30/60/90-day plan — the path forward, with costs

Canadian regulatory coverage

Every charity engagement is framed around the Canadian regulatory environment:

  • PIPEDA — federal privacy legislation, applies to most Canadian charities
  • Quebec Law 25 (formerly Bill 64) — applies to any charity handling personal information about Quebec residents, regardless of charity location
  • Ontario PHIPA — applies to charities handling personal health information
  • CRA charitable status requirements — we factor donor-data handling into the broader compliance picture
  • CyberSecure Canada — federal cybersecurity certification, achievable for a well-prepared charity in 6–12 months; often a path to new funder requirements
